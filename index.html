Social Trends in Different Age Groups

Digital natives, Gen Z are hyper-connected and value diversity, inclusivity, and sustainability. They prioritize experiences over things and are more open about mental health. Short attention spans mean content needs to be quick and engaging.

Often called "digital natives," Millennials are tech-savvy and value work-life balance. They are entrepreneurial and seek meaningful work. This generation is brand loyal and appreciates authenticity.

Independent and resourceful, Gen Xers are often seen as the "sandwich generation" balancing work, family, and aging parents. They value loyalty, hard work, and financial stability. They are practical and prefer direct communication.

Known for their optimism and work ethic, Baby Boomers are often retired or nearing retirement. They value family, tradition, and security. While tech-savvy varies, they appreciate clear and concise information.
